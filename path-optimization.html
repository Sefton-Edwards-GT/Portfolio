Formula 1 Motion Control & Systems Integration

Lab Researcher

Introduction

This project focused on understanding and documenting advanced vehicle trajectory optimization techniques used in autonomous racing. Working under the guidance of Dr. Xiangyu Meng, I analyzed and synthesized complex research to deepen my understanding of path planning and control strategies.

Problem

Autonomous racing requires balancing speed, stability, and smoothness along a track. This creates an optimization challenge that combines vehicle dynamics, control algorithms, and trajectory design. The goal was to understand how research-level models achieve optimal lap times through advanced path optimization methods.

Action

Using insights from the literature and model analysis, I organized and synthesized the information. This involved comparing the performance metrics of different path optimization strategies and interpreting how Catmull-Rom spline-based methods enhanced trajectory generation.

Process

The project began with a detailed review of a 29-page dissertation on vehicle race trajectory optimization. I extracted the key objectives, mathematical formulations, and experimental results that demonstrated performance improvements over standard path planning methods. Next, I examined 11 Simulink models and over 500 lines of MATLAB code to trace how each component, such as path generation, curvature control, and vehicle dynamics, interacted within the simulation environment. Central to the dissertation was the use of Catmull–Rom spline interpolation and a pure pursuit controller, which enabled smooth, continuous-curvature trajectories through defined waypoints. I documented each model’s structure and flow, creating clear summaries and visual mappings of the system’s architecture.

Result

Through this process, I developed a systematic approach to organizing complex technical material and gained practical insights into how trajectory optimization algorithms are modeled and simulated. I strengthened my ability to interpret, synthesize, and communicate technical findings clearly within an academic research context.

Reflection

This project enhanced my understanding of motion control and trajectory planning in autonomous systems. Beyond the technical content, it taught me how to extract key insights from detailed research, bridge theoretical concepts with implementation details, and communicate findings effectively which are foundational skills for future work in autonomous vehicle systems and control engineering.
