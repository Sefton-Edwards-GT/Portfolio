About Me

Electrical Engineering

Introduction

My name is Sefton Edwards, and I am an Electrical Engineering student at the Georgia Institute of Technology with a strong interest in the automotive industry. My goal is utilize my experience and passion for design and engineering to help your organization best meet the goals.

My hometown

I am originally from Baton Rouge, Louisiana, where I began my college education at Louisiana State University. While there, I worked in Dr. Xiangyu Meng’s Electrical and Computer Engineering lab on autonomous vehicle research. That experience sparked my passion for innovation in the automotive field and inspired me to pursue this career path.

My college

Developing a love for research, I decided to transfer to Georgia Tech, my father’s alma mater, to take advantage of its world-class research opportunities in programs such as Vertically Integrated Projects and the Georgia Tech Research Institute. Georgia Tech’s focus on hands-on, interdisciplinary projects allows me to strengthen my technical expertise and explore new areas in control systems and vehicle design.

Campus involvement

I am currently a member of the Phi Sigma Kappa fraternity, which I joined during the Fall 2025 recruitment cycle. Being part of this group has given me a strong sense of community and leadership experience on campus. In addition, I plan to join the Wreck Club during its annual recruitment in the spring. My father was also a member, and like him, I hope to become a Wreck Driver, combining my passion for the automotive industry with family tradition by maintaining and driving our school's mascot, a 1930 Ford Model A Sport coupe.

Special topics

Outside of engineering, I am pursuing a minor in Chinese. Through advanced standing, I placed into Advanced Chinese I, which I am taking in the spring semester. Next year, I plan to study abroad at the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST) to strengthen my language skills, global communication, and cultural understanding.

Future plans

Looking ahead, I hope to continue working on autonomous vehicle technologies and motion control systems through research or within the automotive industry. My long-term goal is to help develop the next generation of transportation systems.
